\begin{abstract}
What kind of human preparation enables meaningful coevolution with AI agents?
This paper reports a longitudinal, autobiographical design study spanning 1,477+ days of daily journaling, 3,300+ interconnected notes, and a month-long intensive collaboration with multiple AI agents across four harness environments.
We identify five dimensions of \textit{preparatory practice}---Rhythm, Association, Reproducibility, Transparency, and Embodiment---and trace how each dimension evolved from theoretical framing to working implementation.
The study contributes (1) a five-dimensional framework for human-agent collaborative readiness, (2) empirical evidence from a single-subject longitudinal design, and (3) an open, reproducible artifact package including the digital garden, agent configuration, and existence-data viewer that constitute the harness itself.
We argue that the bottleneck in human-agent collaboration is not model capability but \textit{the human's preparatory infrastructure}---the harness that turns a session into a relationship.
\end{abstract}

* Introduction

The dominant framing of AI agent collaboration centers on tool capability: better models, richer APIs, more autonomous workflows.
Yet practitioners increasingly report that the decisive factor is not what the model can do, but what the human has prepared for it to work with cite:hashimoto2026harness,anthropic2026context.

This paper asks a different question: /What kind of human preparation enables meaningful coevolution with AI agents?/

We report a longitudinal self-study grounded in autobiographical design cite:neustaedter2012autobiographical---a recognized HCI methodology where the researcher uses their own system over an extended period to generate design knowledge.
Over 1,477+ consecutive days of journaling (2022--present), 3,300+ Denote/Org-mode notes, and a month-long intensive sprint (March 2026) with four AI agents across multiple harness environments, we developed and empirically grounded a five-dimensional framework of /preparatory practice/.

Our contributions are:
1. A *five-dimensional framework* (Rhythm, Association, Reproducibility, Transparency, Embodiment) characterizing the human infrastructure that enables agent collaboration.
2. *Empirical evidence* from a within-subject longitudinal study showing how each dimension evolved from concept to working implementation.
3. An *open artifact package*---digital garden, agent configuration, and existence-data viewer---that constitutes the harness itself and enables inspection of the collaboration process.

* Background and Related Work

** Harness Engineering

Mitchell Hashimoto introduced "Engineer the Harness" as a key phase in AI adoption cite:hashimoto2026harness.
OpenAI formalized harness engineering as the practice of structuring agent environments for reliable operation cite:openai2026harness.
Anthropic characterized context engineering as the successor to prompt engineering cite:anthropic2026context.

We build on this work but shift the focus: while industry harnesses optimize /task performance/, our preparatory practice optimizes /collaborative readiness/---the human's capacity to sustain meaningful interaction across sessions, models, and time.

** Personal Knowledge Management and Digital Gardens

The digital garden movement cite:appleton2020garden treats knowledge as a living, interconnected web rather than a linear publication stream.
Our work extends this tradition by introducing agent-authored notes (botlog) and agent-authored annotations (botment) as first-class layers within the garden.

** Autobiographical Design

Neustaedter and Sengers cite:neustaedter2012autobiographical established autobiographical design as a legitimate HCI research method where "the researcher designs and uses a system themselves over a period of time."
Our study follows this tradition, with the added dimension that the system under study includes AI agents as co-inhabitants.

* The Five Dimensions of Preparatory Practice

** Rhythm
Daily journaling, punchout stamps, agenda protocol.
The temporal backbone that makes sessions into a continuous timeline.

** Association
Denote naming, personal dictionary (dictcli 2,400+ triples), Kiwi morphological analysis, 3-layer semantic search.
The connective tissue that lets agents navigate the human's conceptual web.

** Reproducibility
NixOS declarative environment, shared workflow (workflow-shared.el), common skill set across harnesses, LanceDB memory indices.
Not "anyone can reproduce this" but "the same person and their agents can restart from the same point."

** Transparency
Digital garden (2,100+ public pages), botlog (45+ agent-authored notes), botment (agent annotations), existence-data viewer (geworfen).
Making the process inspectable---for humans, for agents, and for external observers.

** Embodiment
Samsung Health integration (sleep, heart rate, steps, stress), aTimeLogger (18 activity categories), lifetract unified query.
The biological signal layer that closes the loop between cognitive work and physical state.

* Discussion

** Industry Harness vs. Preparatory Practice

The industry harness optimizes /task performance/: "did the agent make fewer mistakes?"
Preparatory practice optimizes /collaborative readiness/: "can the human and agent restart from the same point and go deeper?"

We argue these are complementary layers.
The optimal architecture is industry-standard harness engineering /plus/ preparatory practice on top---what we call the /two-layer harness/.

** Self-Reproducibility, Not Universal Reproducibility

Our reproducibility is not "anyone can replicate this in 10 minutes."
It is "the same person and their agents can restart from the same knowledge, the same timeline, the same conventions."
This distinction matters: the bottleneck is not tooling but /accumulated personal corpus/.

** Limitations

- N=1 study: findings are hypothesis-generating, not generalizable
- The researcher is the designer, user, and evaluator
- Sleep data correlation does not imply causation
- Tools are non-mainstream (NixOS, Emacs, Org-mode)
- Agent outputs may contain biases that reinforce researcher's framing

* Conclusion

We have shown that meaningful human-agent coevolution requires not just capable models but a prepared human---one who has built rhythm, association, reproducibility, transparency, and embodiment into their daily practice.

The harness is not a configuration file.
It is the accumulated infrastructure of a life made legible to machines.
